Cavite’s Breathtaking Mountain Landscapes

Mount Pico de Loro | Cavite & Hamilo Coast in Nasugbu, Batangas.

Rising prominently along the boundary of Cavite and Batangas, Mount Pico de Loro stands as one of the most iconic hiking destinations in the country. Also known as Mount Palay-Palay, this remarkable peak is celebrated for its distinctive parrot-beak-shaped summit and breathtaking panoramic views of lush forests, rolling mountains, and the distant coastline. Located within the protected Mounts Palay-Palay–Mataas-na-Gulod Protected Landscape, it offers both adventure and serenity to nature enthusiasts. Whether for seasoned hikers or beginners seeking a rewarding climb, Mount Pico de Loro provides an unforgettable experience that showcases the natural beauty and ecological richness of Southern Luzon.

Mount Sungay

Eastern Tagaytay | 709 metres (2,326 feet)

Located in the uplands of Cavite, Mount Sungay is one of the highest peaks in the province and a popular destination for those seeking fresh air, scenic views, and a peaceful escape from city life. It is famously known as the site of the iconic “People’s Park in the Sky,” a historic structure perched near its summit that offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding landscape, including nearby provinces and even parts of Metro Manila on clear days.

Mount Marami

Maragondon, Cavite | 405–633 meters

Nestled in the scenic landscapes of Cavite, Mount Marami is a must-visit destination for hiking enthusiasts and nature lovers. Known for its dramatic rock formations, including the iconic “Silyang Bato” (Stone Chair), the mountain offers adventurers a thrilling climb with rewarding panoramic views of lush forests, rolling hills, and nearby towns.
