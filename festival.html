Regada Water Festival

June 24 | Cavite City, Cavite

The Regada Water Festival is a lively celebration held every June 24 in Cavite City, Cavite in honor of Saint John the Baptist. The festival began as a simple religious tradition where people sprinkled water on each other as a symbol of blessing, cleansing, and renewal. Over time, it developed into a vibrant community event where residents and visitors splash water using hoses, buckets, water guns, and even fire trucks while music and street parties fill the city. Today, the Regada Water Festival represents the joy, unity, and strong faith of the people of Cavite City and is considered one of the most exciting festivals in the province.

Wagayway Festival

Col. C. Riel, Maragondon, Cavite

The Wagayway Festival is an annual celebration held in Imus, Cavite every May 28, commemorating the historic Battle of Alapan, where the Philippine flag was first unfurled after a Filipino victory against Spanish forces. The festival showcases the city’s rich history, culture, and patriotism through colorful parades, street performances, and flag-waving activities. Residents and visitors participate in reenactments, musical presentations, and civic programs, reflecting pride in Filipino heritage. The Wagayway Festival not only honors the bravery of revolutionary heroes but also strengthens community spirit and national pride in Cavite.

Paruparu Festival

CW38+J26, Imus, Cavite

The Battle of Alapan took place on May 28, 1898 in Imus, Cavite during the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonial rule. It was one of the first major victories of Filipino revolutionary forces over Spanish troops. The battle was led by Emilio Aguinaldo and his revolutionary soldiers against the Spanish army stationed in Cavite. The Filipino forces successfully defeated the Spanish troops and captured their weapons and ammunition. After the victory, the Philippine flag was first unfurled in triumph in Alapan. This moment symbolized Filipino bravery and the growing strength of the revolution against Spain.
