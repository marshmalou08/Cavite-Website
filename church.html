Guardians of Faith: Cavite’s Religious Heritage

Diocesan Shrine and Parish of Nuestra Señora de Candelaria | Silang, Cavite

The Diocesan Shrine and Parish of Nuestra Señora de Candelaria, located in Silang, Cavite, is a historic and revered Catholic church dedicated to Our Lady of Candelaria. Known for its beautiful architecture and solemn atmosphere, the church serves as a center of devotion and spiritual life for the local community. Every February 2, the feast of Our Lady of Candelaria is celebrated with special masses, processions, and religious activities that attract devotees from all over Cavite. The shrine stands not only as a place of worship but also as a symbol of the enduring faith and cultural heritage of Silang and its people.

Our Lady of the Assumption Parish Church

Col. C. Riel, Maragondon, Cavite

In 1897, after a conflict between revolutionary factions during the Tejeros Convention, Andrés Bonifacio was arrested by forces loyal to Emilio Aguinaldo. He and his brother Procopio Bonifacio were brought to Maragondon where a military court conducted their trial. The trial took place in this house, where Bonifacio was accused of treason and sedition against the revolutionary government. The court later found him guilty, and he was sentenced to death.

St. Mary Magdalene Parish Church

Kawit, Cavite

The Battle of Alapan took place on May 28, 1898 in Imus, Cavite during the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonial rule. It was one of the first major victories of Filipino revolutionary forces over Spanish troops. The battle was led by Emilio Aguinaldo and his revolutionary soldiers against the Spanish army stationed in Cavite. The Filipino forces successfully defeated the Spanish troops and captured their weapons and ammunition. After the victory, the Philippine flag was first unfurled in triumph in Alapan. This moment symbolized Filipino bravery and the growing strength of the revolution against Spain.
